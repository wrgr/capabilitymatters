About this site

The LENS program, in its own voice — not a field-wide index.

This site covers Johns Hopkins' LENS (Learning Engineering for Next-Generation Systems) specialization: the program itself, field notes written in the LENS voice, and case material like the LLM101 exemplar. It doesn't try to index the broader field — for the shared reading list, tools, practice library, events calendar, and community roster, see the Learning Engineering Commons, most of it curated from the IEEE ICICLE community.

Where it applies

LENS focuses on high-consequence, high-impact domains — where human capability is inseparable from system performance and the cost of getting it wrong is measured in lives, readiness, and equity. Seven recur across the program's case material and teaching: healthcare, defense & national security, education at scale, aviation, energy, manufacturing & industrial, and government & public infrastructure.

People & programs

LENS is a concentration within the JHU MEd in Learning Design and Technology, jointly run by the Johns Hopkins School of Education and the Applied Physics Laboratory, with an IEEE ICICLE partnership. Faculty in the concentration include James Diamond and William Gray-Roncal (School of Education / APL); Adesola Ogundimu co-chairs IEEE ICICLE's Globalisation/Localisation SIG. Full profiles for these and other practitioners across the field live at lecommons's community pages.

Scope & caveats

  • LENS-focused, not comprehensive — on purpose. This site stays narrow; it doesn't try to be a complete index of the field. For that, see lecommons.
  • ICICLE is authoritative. When ICICLE and this site differ on a definition or taxonomy, defer to ICICLE. This site doesn't claim to speak for IEEE ICICLE or host its content — it connects to it.
  • Links may rot. External resources change. File an issue if you find one.

Tools used

Claude (Anthropic) was used as an assistant throughout development and content curation — for code generation, draft copy, and triage of the underlying material. Final editorial decisions, scope, and accuracy review are human-authored.
