For decades, aviation's deadliest problem wasn't broken machines — it was capable crews failing to combine what they each knew. Crew Resource Management made teamwork itself an engineered system: explicit protocols for how a cockpit shares information, challenges a captain, and manages workload under stress. Paired with the industry's data-driven CAST process, it helped cut U.S. commercial fatality risk by 83%. It is the counter-case to the MAX in the same domain — capability built into the seams between people, on purpose.
