The Macondo blowout killed eleven workers and became the largest marine oil spill in U.S. history — and the National Commission traced the root cause to systematic risk-management failures across BP, Halliburton, and Transocean. The crew misread the negative-pressure test, the one check of whether the well was sealed, and kept working a well that wasn't. Every defense — procedure, training, supervision, contractor coordination, equipment — had drifted independently until none could catch the others. It is the book's canonical multi-layer failure, and its sharpest warning that an industry-leading injury record can be a measurement of the wrong thing.
