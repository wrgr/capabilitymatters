The andon cord is the most studied piece of string in industrial history: pull it, and the assembly line stops. What makes it work isn't the cord — it's that a front-line worker has the standing to halt a multimillion-dollar line the moment they see a defect, and the expectation that they will. Defects get fixed at the source instead of propagating downstream. Like Keystone's nurse authority, it is capability engineered as permission — the system trusts the person closest to the problem to act on it.
