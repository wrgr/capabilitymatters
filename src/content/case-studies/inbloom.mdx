inBloom is the rare failure with no bug, no breach, no performance problem. The $100-million student-data platform worked exactly as built. What killed it in about fourteen months was everything around the technology: it launched with no consent framework, no community engagement, no way for a parent to have a say in how their child's data moved. Nine states withdrew. It is the book's clearest argument that in education at scale, stakeholder trust and governance are not features you add later — they are load-bearing structure, and their absence is a design defect.
